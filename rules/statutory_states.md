# Statutory States: Non-Compete Enforcement Rules

## Classification
These states enforce non-compete agreements ONLY if the employee meets specific statutory thresholds, such as minimum salary or minimum notice requirements.

## Key Jurisdictions
- **Washington**: Wash. Rev. Code § 19.86.140 — Non-competes enforceable only for employees earning $100,000+ (indexed annually).
- **Illinois**: 740 Ill. Comp. Stat. 140/2 — Non-competes enforceable only for employees earning $75,000+ (indexed annually); requires written notice at time of employment or receipt of a "tangible benefit."

## Compliance Strategy

### DO:
1. **Verify Salary Threshold**: Before enforcing a non-compete, confirm the employee's salary meets or exceeds the statutory minimum.
   - Washington: $100,000+ (2024)
   - Illinois: $75,000+ (2024)
2. **Provide Written Notice**: Ensure the non-compete is provided in writing at the time of hire or during employment with a concurrent "tangible benefit" (bonus, promotion, etc.).
3. **Document the Benefit**: If providing the non-compete after hire, document what consideration is given (continued employment may not suffice).
4. **Use a Template**: Ensure language is compliant with the specific statute and includes all required elements.
5. **Combine with Other Protections**: Use confidentiality and non-solicitation clauses as well.

### DO NOT:
1. Enforce a non-compete against an employee earning below the statutory threshold.
2. Assume "continued employment" is sufficient consideration in these states.
3. Impose non-competes without written notice.

## Model Clause for Statutory States (Illinois Example)

```
NON-COMPETE AGREEMENT (STATUTORY STATES)

This Non-Compete Agreement is entered into as of [DATE] between [COMPANY] ("Company") 
and [EMPLOYEE] ("Employee").

RECITALS:
WHEREAS, Employee is being employed by Company at an annual salary of $[AMOUNT], which 
exceeds the statutory threshold under 740 Ill. Comp. Stat. 140/2;

WHEREAS, Employee is receiving [describe tangible benefit: stock options, sign-on bonus, 
promotion, etc.] as consideration for entering into this Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, Employee agrees that during employment and for [12-24] months thereafter, 
Employee will not:

(a) Engage in any business that directly competes with Company's business in the 
    [specified geographic area];
(b) Solicit customers or employees of Company;
(c) Disclose or use confidential information.

This Agreement is necessary to protect Company's legitimate business interests and is 
enforceable under applicable state law.
```

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Employee salary meets or exceeds statutory threshold
- [ ] Written notice provided at time of hire (or updated employment agreement with tangible benefit)
- [ ] Clear geographic and temporal restrictions (typically 12-24 months, defined radius)
- [ ] Legitimate business interest documented
- [ ] Compliance with state-specific statutory language

## Enforcement Notes

- **Strict Compliance Required**: Courts in these states are exacting about statutory compliance.
- **Salary Inflation**: Thresholds are typically indexed annually; verify current minimum each year.
- **Tangible Benefit**: If providing non-compete after hire, a "tangible benefit" must be given (signed bonus, promotion, stock options, training, etc.). "Continued employment" alone is generally insufficient.

## Red Flags

- Non-compete for employee below salary threshold (likely unenforceable).
- No documented tangible benefit when non-compete provided after hire.
- No written notice at time of employment.
- Overreaching geographic or temporal restrictions.
