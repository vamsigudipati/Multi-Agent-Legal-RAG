# Prohibitive States: Non-Compete Enforcement Rules

## Classification
These states largely prohibit or severely restrict employee non-compete agreements, with narrow exceptions only for sale of business or partnership dissolution.

## Key Jurisdictions
- **California**: Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 16600 — "Every contract by which anyone is restrained from engaging in a lawful profession, trade, or business of any kind is to that extent void."
- **Oklahoma**: Okla. Stat. tit. 15, § 219 — Void except in narrow circumstances (sale of goodwill, partnership dissolution).

## Compliance Strategy

### DO:
1. **Confidentiality Agreements**: Clearly define what information is confidential (trade secrets, client lists, pricing, strategic plans).
2. **Invention Assignment Agreements**: Require assignment of all inventions created during employment, especially those related to company business.
3. **Non-Solicitation Agreements**: Restrict solicitation of employees and customers (often enforceable where non-competes are not).
4. **Garden Leave Clauses** (with caution): Paid leave during notice periods can substitute for non-competes in limited cases.
5. **Nondisclosure Agreements (NDAs)**: Lifetime protection for trade secrets.

### DO NOT:
1. Rely on non-compete clauses for employee restraint.
2. Include geographic or time-based restrictions on employment.
3. Assert that non-competes are enforceable (courts will strike them down).

## Model Clause for Prohibitive States

```
CONFIDENTIALITY AND INVENTION ASSIGNMENT

Employee agrees to:
(a) Maintain the confidentiality of all proprietary information disclosed during employment.
(b) Assign all inventions, discoveries, and intellectual property developed during the term of employment (or within 6 months thereafter, if related to Company business) to Company.
(c) Not disclose or use confidential information for any purpose other than performing duties for Company.
(d) Return all confidential materials upon termination.
```

## Red Flags

- Any clause that restricts where an employee may work after employment ends.
- Any clause that restricts the type of work an employee may perform.
- Unreasonable notice periods in "garden leave" provisions.
