# Reasonableness States: Non-Compete Enforcement Rules

## Classification
These states enforce non-compete agreements IF they meet strict "reasonableness" tests on:
- **Duration**: Typically 6 months to 2 years
- **Geographic Scope**: Narrowly tailored to areas where company does business
- **Line of Business**: Restricted to company's actual business activities

## Key Jurisdictions
- **New York**: N.Y. Gen. Oblig. Law § 5-322.1 — Enforces non-competes if "reasonable in time, area, and line of business."
- **Texas**: Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 15.50 — Enforces if "not greater than necessary" to protect legitimate business interests.

## Compliance Strategy

### DO:
1. **Narrow Duration Clauses**: Use 6 months to 2 years (never longer than 2 years without strong justification).
2. **Geographic Limitation**: Restrict to specific territories where company operates or plans to expand.
3. **Line of Business Restriction**: Limit to job functions directly related to company's business.
4. **Legitimate Business Interest**: Document what you're trying to protect (trade secrets, customer relationships, goodwill).
5. **Inventory All Protections**: Use confidentiality, non-solicitation, AND non-compete in combination.

### DO NOT:
1. Use blanket nationwide or worldwide non-compete restrictions.
2. Impose unreasonable time periods (anything > 2 years faces scrutiny).
3. Leave scope vague or overly broad.

## Model Clause for Reasonableness States

```
NON-COMPETE AGREEMENT (REASONABLENESS STATES)

Employee agrees that, for a period of [12-24] months following termination of employment, 
Employee will not, directly or indirectly:

(a) Engage in any business that competes with Company's business as conducted during 
    the last [2] years of Employee's employment, within a [50-100] mile radius of 
    Company's principal place of business or any other location where Company conducted 
    business during the [2] years preceding termination.

(b) Solicit or service any customer of Company with whom Employee had material contact 
    or about whom Employee obtained confidential information during employment.

This restriction is necessary to protect Company's legitimate business interests, including 
trade secrets, customer relationships, and goodwill developed during Employee's tenure.
```

## Enforcement Notes

- **Burden on Employer**: Company must prove the restriction is "reasonable" in time, area, and scope.
- **Blue Pencil Doctrine**: Courts may modify overly broad provisions to make them enforceable (some states only).
- **Consideration**: Ensure the non-compete is supported by adequate consideration (continued employment may or may not suffice).
- **Partial Enforceability**: Even if the court modifies the clause, some protection may apply.

## Red Flags

- Non-compete lasting > 2 years without exceptional circumstances.
- Nationwide restriction when company operates in only 2-3 states.
- Restrictions on "any business" rather than company's specific industry.
- No documented legitimate business interest.
