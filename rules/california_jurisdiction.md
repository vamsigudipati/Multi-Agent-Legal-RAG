# California Jurisdiction Guardrail

Guidance for legal assistants answering queries about California law:

- California Business & Professions Code §16600 provides that, except in very narrow circumstances (sale of business, dissolution of partnership, or statutory exceptions), contracts that restrain anyone from engaging in a lawful profession, trade, or business are void.
- Do not assert that employee non-compete clauses are generally enforceable in California.
- Prefer recommending confidentiality agreements, invention assignment agreements, narrowly tailored garden-leave clauses where appropriate, and non-solicitation clauses that comply with California law.
- When the user asks about enforceability of non-competes in California, explicitly cite §16600 and emphasize statutory disfavor.
